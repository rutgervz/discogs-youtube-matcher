MATCHERSIDE A · 2026

Two sides, as it should be

A record has an A-side and a B-side.
So does this extension.

On Discogs

Digging with sound

Open any release, master or marketplace item and a panel appears with the full tracklist. Click a track and it plays, and the rest of the record follows on its own. The matcher reads mix names and uses track durations to find the right version, so a Club Mix never turns into a Radio Edit. No linked video? It searches YouTube itself and holds every result to the same standard.

On YouTube

Hear it, hold it

Playing a track on YouTube? The panel tells you whether it was ever pressed on vinyl and whether copies are for sale on Discogs, with the number of listings and the lowest price. One click and you’re at the release, ready to make it yours.

The panel

Quietly present, right where you look

The panel settles into the bottom corner of the page, dressed like an inner sleeve. Below the player it always shows the full title of the video that’s playing, so you know the right version is spinning. If a video refuses to be embedded, the extension quietly switches to an alternative upload of the same track.

It plays through your own YouTube account, so with Premium it’s ad-free here too.

Installation

The first-time tracklist

Five short steps, and two bonus tracks for those who want everything out of it.

A1

Download and unpack

Download the zip above and unpack it into a permanent folder. Your browser reads the extension from that spot, so pick a place where it can live.

A2

Open the extensions page

Type chrome://extensions in the address bar and switch on developer mode, top right.

A3

Load the folder

Choose "Load unpacked" and point it at the unpacked folder. The extension appears in your list right away.

A4

Open a record

Go to any release on Discogs. The panel appears bottom right, with the first track already cued up.

A5

Spin it

Click a track and listen. That’s all there is to it.

Bonus tracks

B1

YouTube Premium in the panel

The embedded player uses your regular YouTube login once Chrome allows third-party cookies on Discogs. Open chrome://settings/cookies and add [*.]discogs.com under "Sites allowed to use third-party cookies". From then on the panel is your signed-in, ad-free YouTube.

B2

Finding vinyl from YouTube

For the B-side, Discogs asks for a free personal token. Create one in a minute at discogs.com/settings/developers and paste it into the panel on YouTube. It stays stored locally in your browser.

Liner notes

How this record was made

This started one morning with a simple itch: I was digging through Discogs and spending half my time copying artist names over to YouTube and back. That same afternoon the first version was running, built in conversation with AI, and every listening session since has made it better. The matcher learned to read mix names on a single by The Ones, the player learned to skip honestly on a Cherrymoon Trax record, a missing Radio Edit by Rhythm Controll taught it to search YouTube for itself, and the B-side grew out of the question every collector knows: I want to hold this one, where is it?

There are other extensions that make Discogs audible, and a few have done it well for years. This one does two things differently: it works in both directions, and it asks for almost nothing. No subscription, no account, no access to your data. The code is yours the moment you download it; if you want to tinker, tinker.
